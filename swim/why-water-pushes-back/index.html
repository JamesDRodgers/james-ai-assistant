The Core Principle

Drag is not one thing. It is four forces working against you simultaneously and they are all connected: Form Drag, Boundary Layer Separation, Active Drag, and Wave Drag. Fix your body position and you address all four at once.

⬛
Form Drag
The Wall You Build
▾

Your body casts a shadow in the water. The bigger that shadow, the harder water pushes back. A dropped hip or raised head doubles that shadow instantly and you feel it as pure resistance.

Feel It
Swimming uphill. Legs heavy from the first stroke.
Fix It
Horizontal body. Hips at the surface. Head neutral.
🌊
Boundary Layer
Losing the Water's Cooperation
▾

Water wants to flow smoothly along your body. When something disrupts that - a jutting elbow, a collapsing core, a wide kick - the flow detaches and creates turbulent pockets of resistance behind you.

Feel It
Sudden heaviness mid-length. Like the water grabs you.
Fix It
Tight core. Narrow kick. Clean elbow path.
💪
Active Drag
The Real Number
▾

Passive drag is measured towing a rigid body. The moment you start stroking and breathing, the drag picture changes completely. Every arm recovery, every breath, every kick cycle compounds the resistance.

Feel It
Working hard, going slow. Effort and speed do not match.
Fix It
Technique over strength. Every. Single. Time.
〰️
Wave Drag
Energy Spent Going Nowhere
▾

Every wave your body makes at the surface costs energy that never comes back as forward motion. Head position is the primary culprit - looking forward creates a bow wave that lifts your front and sinks your hips.

Feel It
A wall of water in front of you. Breathing feels like climbing.
Fix It
Eyes down. Head neutral. Get deep off every wall.
How They Connect

Poor position creates a bigger frontal shadow, which causes flow to separate earlier, which creates more waves at the surface, all of which compounds as active drag. These are not four problems. They are one system.

The Silence Drill

Swim one length making as little noise and splash as possible.

Your body will instinctively lower the head, tighten the kick, clean up the entry, and engage the core. Then you will understand why it worked - because you already felt it.
