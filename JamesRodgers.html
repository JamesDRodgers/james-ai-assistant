About

I don't have a
simple job title.

I'm an ordained minister, educator, and generative AI specialist whose work has ranged from teaching third grade through Teach For America to contributing to the development of Grok at xAI.

I've taught third grade in Jacksonville through Teach For America, rebuilt a band program in an underserved suburb of Cincinnati, led a church community through the pandemic, contributed to the development of Grok at xAI, and built AI-supported learning and coaching tools for competitive swimming.

The through-line in my career isn't the industry. It's the way I approach the relationship between learning, learners, and organizational culture.

I help organizations close the gap between vision and day-to-day practice.

For the past 15 years, I've designed learning systems that improve performance, strengthen communication, and help people apply what they've learned.

How I approach the work

I tend to start by understanding the environment around the work: the people involved, how communication happens, the constraints teams are working under, and the obstacles getting in the way. Learning design that ignores those conditions rarely sticks.

I see artificial intelligence as a practical tool that supports human judgment, creativity, and decision-making rather than replacing it. That's the perspective I bring to building AI-integrated learning experiences: not automation, but something that makes people better at what they already do.
