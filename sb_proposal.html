Concept 00

Groundwork

Practice to protect real data.

Workflow

Calibration → Synthetic Document → Leader Flags Issues → Guided Fix → Personal Checklist

Overview

Groundwork is a rehearsal space, not a redaction tool. It generates synthetic messy documents. The leader, not the bot, catches what's wrong and proposes the fix. No real organizational data enters the chat.

Opportunity

Nonprofit data is often messy and inconsistently handled, and confidentiality mistakes are easy to make by accident. This is one of the sector's most invisible skill gaps, and staff experience ranges from never having cleaned a spreadsheet to formal data training.

How It Works

  • A short calibration sets starting difficulty based on prior experience
  • Leader selects a document type: intake form, donor spreadsheet, board memo, case notes
  • Groundwork Bot fabricates a synthetic version seeded with structural issues (duplicates, missing fields, misaligned columns) and confidentiality issues (names, dates of birth, financial or case details)
  • Leader flags what they'd catch before it reaches an AI tool; the bot nudges toward anything missed, without naming it outright for advanced users
  • Leader proposes the fix and sees the corrected version beside the original
  • Session ends with a checklist built from what the leader actually caught

AI Skills Developed

Foundational — sets the baseline for every concept that follows

Confidentiality judgment Structural vs. unstructured data Garbage-in/garbage-out reasoning Human-in-the-loop verification

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Leaders return to build proficiency across structured and unstructured data. Confidentiality is one of the leading reasons nonprofit leaders hesitate to use AI, and Groundwork is the practice ground for that concern.

Value to Springboard AI

Groundwork is a credibility layer. It keeps bad data from being blamed on AI, and gives leaders at any skill level a confident starting point.

Considerations

Effectiveness depends on how well synthetic documents mirror real data. Groundwork is rehearsal, not verification: it does not check real documents, and that responsibility stays with the leader.

Optional Future Expansion

Organization-specific templates reflecting a leader's actual data types Periodic recertification with new scenarios

Concept 01

Amplify

Turn this month's impact into a message that reaches the audiences that matter.

Workflow

Board Report → AI Extracts → Leader Reviews → Audience Planning → 7-Day Communication Plan

Overview

Amplify produces a communication plan, not a single campaign. It transforms a board report or impact document into a full week of audience-ready content in under thirty minutes, without requiring any communications staff.

Opportunity

Nonprofits produce real impact monthly but rarely have the staff or time to communicate it consistently. Donor emails go unsent. Social feeds go dark. Funders go without updates. The impact exists. Amplify helps share it.

How It Works

  • Upload a board report, impact summary, or program update
  • Amplify surfaces what it found: numbers, stories, milestones, challenges
  • Leader confirms, corrects, or prioritizes the extracted content
  • Answer three questions: audience, desired outcome, anything timely to build around
  • Amplify generates a seven-day communication plan with drafted content for each slot

AI Skills Developed

Foundational concepts — introduced here for the first time

Prompt grounding Audience-specific prompt construction Prompt chaining Iterative refinement Prompt portability

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Board reports arrive monthly. Each one is a new reason to open Amplify. A leader who saves three hours tells their ED. The platform gains a second user who didn't attend the training.

Value to Springboard AI

Amplify turns communicating impact into a repeatable, low-friction workflow grounded in what the organization already produces. It shows that AI-assisted communication does not have to sound generic.

Considerations

Review all output before it reaches donors, funders, or board members, and remove client-identifiable details from source documents before uploading.

Optional Future Expansion

Saved organization voice profile for consistent tone across sessions Shared team access for communications staff or volunteers

Concept 02

Core

Output grounded in the strength of your organization's mission.

Workflow

Mission · Vision · Plan → Organizational Context → Any AI Prompt → Grounded Output

Overview

Core lets a leader upload their mission, vision, and strategic plan once. These documents feed a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) system: relevant context is pulled from them for each request, then used to generate a response, so output like grant narratives, board reports, and funder letters draws on the organization's own language instead of a generic response.

Opportunity

Generic AI output has no organizational context, so it reads as competent but hollow. It doesn't reflect the organization's mission, voice, or priorities, and staff can't easily verify what the AI knows about the organization when generating that output.

How It Works

  • Upload mission statement, vision, and strategic plan
  • Issue any prompt: grant narrative, board update, funder letter
  • Compare grounded output against a generic response to see the difference directly

AI Skills Developed

Builds on Amplify — adds organizational grounding and RAG

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) Context window awareness Grounding vs. hallucination System prompts and custom instructions

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Once organizational documents are uploaded, future tasks on the platform benefit from them.

Value to Springboard AI

Core is the organizational intelligence layer that makes every other platform asset more precise. It teaches RAG through use, not instruction, and makes the platform purpose-built for each organization. Mission becomes the engine.

Considerations

Uploaded documents should be reviewed before submission. Strategic plans may contain confidential financial projections, staffing decisions, or donor information.

If organizational documents contain outdated language or unexamined assumptions, Core will reflect those patterns. Document quality directly affects output quality.

Optional Future Expansion

Cross-organization partnership alignment checks Institutional memory for staff onboarding

Concept 03

Illuminate

Bring the human story inside your budget into the light.

Workflow

Budget Categories + Org Documents → AI Matching → Story · Source → Narrative Budget

Overview

Illuminate matches budget categories to real stories already sitting in the organization's own documents, producing a narrative budget that shows how spending aligns with mission and makes financial data legible to donors, boards, and funders.

Opportunity

A budget is a spreadsheet: accurate, necessary, and invisible to anyone who cannot picture what a category funds in the world. A narrative budget connects spending to mission and human outcome. Those stories already exist.

Illuminate is contextual by design. It matches budget categories only to stories that exist in the organization's own documents, flags every match with its source, and states plainly when no honest match exists. It will not invent.

How It Works

  • Upload a budget alongside existing organizational documents in any combination
  • Illuminate reads all documents and extracts stories, statistics, quotes, and outcomes
  • Stories are matched and grouped by budget category, not individual line item
  • Unmatched categories are flagged honestly rather than filled with invented content
  • Output is a narrative budget organized by category, showing amount, matched story, source, and how the spending supports the mission

AI Skills Developed

Builds on Amplify and Core — adds multi-document synthesis and guardrails

Multi-document retrieval Task decomposition Guardrails Source attribution AI transparency

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Budgets are revised for fiscal year close, capital campaigns, and major appeals. Illuminate has a recurring trigger built into the organization's own financial calendar, not a platform reminder.

Value to Springboard AI

Illuminate serves the sector's top AI use cases, grant writing and donor engagement, through a format most organizations have never produced efficiently. The integrity concern is answered in the architecture: no fabrication, full source attribution, honest when matches do not exist.

Considerations

Case notes, intake forms, and client-facing documents should be reviewed and anonymized before upload to protect client confidentiality.

Match quality depends on the completeness of source documents. Illuminate will not fill gaps with invented content.

Optional Future Expansion

Integration with common accounting tools Multi-year narrative tracking Funder-specific narrative templates

Concept 04

Coach

An interactive, dynamic prompt library for real nonprofit work.

Instructional Sequence

Real Task → Coach Demonstrates → Work Together → Leader Owns It → Saved to Library

Overview

Coach is used for brainstorming and drafting prompts. It functions as an interactive, dynamic prompt library: leaders build prompts for real work, save them, and draw on prompts contributed by other leaders through Leap.

Opportunity

The gap between training inspiration and workplace execution is wider than a 90-minute session can bridge. Generic prompts produce generic output. A two-person housing nonprofit needs a different prompt than a fifty-person youth development organization. Coach closes that gap by meeting leaders at their actual work.

How It Works

Coach demonstrates

  • Nonprofit leader arrives with something that needs to be produced or a task they want to brainstorm: a grant narrative, board report, or donor email
  • Coach asks three questions: what is the organization trying to do, who is this for, what does a good result look like
  • Coach builds a prompt, shows it, and runs it immediately
  • Leader sees the output before any explanation is given

Coach and leader work together

  • Coach names what happened: what each prompt element did, what the AI was instructed to do, and why the output took the shape it did
  • Leader adjusts an element (audience, tone, format, or context) and sees what changes

Leader owns it

  • Leader refines and regenerates independently
  • The finished prompt is saved to the leader's library, alongside relevant prompts contributed by other organizations through Leap

AI Skills Developed

Synthesizes concepts introduced in Amplify, Core, and Illuminate, and names them explicitly for the first time

Prompt anatomy Prompt portability Output evaluation Reflective practice

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Every new task needs its own prompt: a grant narrative and a board report don't share one. Leaders come back each time real work comes up, and the library grows with each new submission to Leap, giving them more to draw on with each visit.

Value to Springboard AI

Coach is where training transfer happens. It applies the same instructional sequence as the live session (try first, understand after) to real work the leader already needs to do. Each session produces a saved, reusable prompt rather than a one-time output.

Considerations

Coach produces drafts, not final documents. All output should be reviewed for accuracy before use.

Describing organizational context conversationally can inadvertently include sensitive information. Leaders should be mindful of what they share in the session.

Optional Future Expansion

Session history and progress tracking Browsing the library by category, role, or task type

Concept 05

Leap

One idea shared moves the whole sector forward.

Agentic Workflow

Submission → AI Review → Human Review → Prompt Library → Coach

Overview

Users submit AI wins, prompts, and artifacts to Leap. An AI agent reviews each submission against defined criteria and routes approved entries to a human curator. Approved submissions are organized into an interactive prompt library, which Coach draws on to help leaders with their own projects.

Opportunity

Generic AI examples do not resonate with nonprofit leaders. A housing organization learns differently from a housing example than from a corporate one. No existing platform captures real, sector-specific AI wins at scale and makes them retrievable by context.

Submitting real work raises legitimate concerns: confidentiality, rejection, and competitive exposure. Leap addresses each structurally. A confidentiality check runs before any submission enters the queue, and returned submissions come with specific feedback rather than a flat rejection.

How It Works

  • Leader submits an AI win, prompt, or artifact through a structured form: organization context, challenge faced, AI tool used, prompt, artifact, and outcome
  • Submissions without a formal prompt are also accepted
  • An AI agent scores the submission and returns one of three outcomes: approved, returned for revision with specific feedback, or discarded with explanation
  • Approved submissions pass to a human curator for final review
  • Approved submissions are added to the prompt library, tagged by context, tool, and use case
  • Coach draws on the library to surface relevant prompts for leaders working on similar projects

AI Skills Developed

Most advanced concept in the set — introduces agentic AI and community knowledge systems

Agentic AI Prompt quality and construction RAG at scale Responsible AI use Human oversight

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Leaders return to check on submissions, to use Coach with new library entries, and to submit their next win.

Value to Springboard AI

Leap makes Coach more useful over time. Each approved submission adds to the prompt library. Approved submissions are also a record of applied AI use, which Springboard AI can report to funders.

Considerations

A confidentiality check runs before any submission enters the review queue, but leaders carry primary responsibility for what they submit.

The library will initially be sparse. Seeding it with curated submissions from trusted organizations before launch is recommended.

Submissions should be subject to a clear retention and deletion policy. Leaders should be able to remove their contributions at any time.

Optional Future Expansion

Browsing the library by category or role Analytics on which prompts Coach surfaces most often Funder-facing view of aggregate AI adoption patterns across grantee organizations

Future Design Recommendation

Learning Outcomes and Assessment

A shared outcomes framework would help Springboard AI evaluate genuine capability growth rather than activity completion. The following six competencies reflect where nonprofit AI leadership is heading in 2026, durable enough to outlast any specific tool.

AI Literacy

Understand capabilities, limitations, and vocabulary.

Workflow Design

Identify where AI creates the most organizational value.

Prompting and Agents

Move from asking questions to orchestrating work.

Governance and Ethics

Protect trust, privacy, and mission integrity.

Organizational Change

Help staff adopt AI effectively and responsibly.

Strategic Leadership

Decide where AI advances the mission, and where humans must remain central.

Performance-based assessment via Leap: The concepts build these competencies through authentic, task-based practice. Participants demonstrate understanding by producing artifacts, not completing quizzes. Leap offers the platform's strongest opportunity for performance-based assessment by capturing prompts, revisions, and completed work over time.
