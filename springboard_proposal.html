Concept 01

Amplify

Turn this month's impact into a message that reaches the audiences that matter.

Workflow

Board Report → AI Extracts → Leader Reviews → Audience Planning → 7-Day Campaign

Overview

Amplify transforms a board report or impact document into a full week of audience-ready communications in under thirty minutes, without requiring any communications staff.

Opportunity

Nonprofits produce real impact monthly but rarely have the staff or time to communicate it consistently. Donor emails go unsent. Social feeds go dark. Funders go without updates. The impact exists. The infrastructure to share it does not.

How It Works

  • Upload a board report, impact summary, or program update
  • Amplify surfaces what it found: numbers, stories, milestones, challenges
  • Leader confirms, corrects, or prioritizes the extracted content
  • Answer three questions: audience, desired outcome, anything timely to build around
  • Amplify generates a seven-day plan with drafted content for each slot
  • Review, edit inline, and export or schedule

AI Skills Developed

Foundational concepts, introduced here for the first time

Prompt grounding Audience-specific prompt construction Prompt chaining Iterative refinement Prompt portability

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Board reports arrive monthly. Each one is a new reason to open Amplify. A leader who saves three hours tells their ED. The platform gains a second user who didn't attend the training.

Value to SpringboardAI

Amplify turns a recurring organizational task, communicating impact, into a repeatable, low-friction workflow grounded in what the organization already produces. It builds a communications habit without requiring new behavior to adopt it. For staff who worry AI will depersonalize their voice, Amplify demonstrates the opposite.

Considerations

Review all output before it reaches donors, funders, or board members, and remove client-identifiable details from source documents before uploading.

Optional Future Expansion

Saved organization voice profile for consistent tone across sessions Multi-platform scheduling integration Shared team access for communications staff or volunteers

Concept 02

Core

Output grounded in the strength of your organization's mission.

Workflow

Mission · Vision · Plan → Organizational Context → Any AI Prompt → Grounded Output

Overview

Core lets a leader upload their mission, vision, and strategic plan once, so subsequent output, grant narratives, board reports, funder letters, reflects who the organization actually is rather than what a generic chatbot produces.

Opportunity

Generic AI output sounds competent and hollow: it has no organizational context. Under funding pressure, that gap compounds quietly. Mission language drifts, voice becomes generic, and output that could have represented the organization ends up representing no one in particular.

Nonprofits are better positioned to solve this than most organizations realize. A mission that means something, a vision that predates any funding cycle, values that do not shift when the market does, these are exactly what AI needs to produce output that is specific rather than hollow. Core makes that foundation the basis for every interaction on the platform. As boards extend governance expectations to include AI, it also gives organizations a structural answer to what their AI is authorized to know.

How It Works

  • Upload mission statement, vision, and strategic plan
  • Issue any prompt: grant narrative, board update, funder letter
  • Compare grounded output against a generic response to see the difference directly
  • Opt in to a shared ecosystem of peer organization documents for partnership and alignment checks

AI Skills Developed

Builds on Amplify, adds organizational grounding and RAG

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) Context window awareness Grounding vs. hallucination System prompts and custom instructions

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Once organizational documents are uploaded, future tasks on the platform benefit from them. Leaving means starting over. Core creates platform dependency that deepens with use.

Value to SpringboardAI

Core is the organizational intelligence layer that makes every other platform asset more precise. It teaches RAG through use, not instruction, and makes the platform purpose-built for each organization. Mission becomes the engine.

Considerations

Uploaded documents should be reviewed before submission. Strategic plans may contain confidential financial projections, staffing decisions, or donor information.

If organizational documents contain outdated language or unexamined assumptions, Core will reflect those patterns. Document quality directly affects output quality.

Optional Future Expansion

Cross-organization partnership alignment checks Institutional memory for staff onboarding

Concept 03

Illuminate

Bring the human story inside a budget line into the light.

Workflow

Budget Line Items + Org Documents → AI Matching → Story · Source → Narrative Budget

Overview

Illuminate matches each line item in a budget to a real story already sitting in the organization's own documents, producing a narrative budget that makes financial data legible to donors, boards, and funders.

Opportunity

A budget is a spreadsheet: accurate, necessary, and invisible to anyone who cannot picture what a line item does in the world. A narrative budget connects each dollar to a human outcome. Those stories already exist.

Illuminate is contextual by design. It matches budget lines only to stories that exist in the organization's own documents, flags every match with its source, and states plainly when no honest match exists. It will not invent.

How It Works

  • Upload budget line items alongside existing organizational documents in any combination
  • Illuminate reads all documents and extracts stories, statistics, quotes, and outcomes
  • Each budget category is matched to the strongest relevant story found
  • Unmatched lines are flagged honestly rather than filled with invented content
  • Output is a structured table: budget category, amount, story match, source, narrative bridge sentence
  • Export for use in fundraising and board materials

AI Skills Developed

Builds on Amplify and Core, adds multi-document synthesis and guardrails

Multi-document retrieval Task decomposition Guardrails Source attribution AI transparency

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Budgets are revised for fiscal year close, capital campaigns, and major appeals. Illuminate has a recurring trigger built into the organization's own financial calendar, not a platform reminder.

Value to SpringboardAI

Illuminate serves the sector's top AI use cases, grant writing and donor engagement, through a format most organizations have never produced efficiently. The integrity concern is answered in the architecture: no fabrication, full source attribution, honest when matches do not exist.

Considerations

Case notes, intake forms, and client-facing documents should be reviewed and anonymized before upload to protect client confidentiality.

Match quality depends on the completeness of source documents. Illuminate will not fill gaps with invented content.

Optional Future Expansion

Integration with common accounting tools Multi-year narrative tracking Funder-specific narrative templates

Concept 04

Coach

Build AI fluency, one task at a time.

Instructional Sequence

Real Task → Coach Demonstrates → Work Together → Leader Owns It → Transferable Skill

Overview

Coach mirrors the pedagogical structure of the live training, active use before explanation, to help leaders build contextual prompts for their real work and walk away with both a usable artifact and transferable AI skills.

Opportunity

The gap between training inspiration and workplace execution is wider than a 90-minute session can bridge. Generic prompts produce generic output. A two-person housing nonprofit needs a different prompt than a fifty-person youth development organization. Coach closes that gap by meeting leaders at their actual work and teaching AI fluency as a byproduct of getting something real done.

How It Works

Coach demonstrates

  • Leader arrives with something that needs to be produced: a grant narrative, board report, or donor email
  • Coach asks three questions: what is the organization trying to do, who is this for, what does a good result look like
  • Coach builds a prompt, shows it, and runs it immediately
  • Leader sees the output before any explanation is given

Coach and leader work together

  • Coach names what happened: what each prompt element did, what the AI was instructed to do, and why the output took the shape it did
  • Leader adjusts an element, audience, tone, format, or context, and sees what changes

Leader owns it

  • Leader refines and regenerates independently
  • Leaves with a usable artifact and a prompt they understand well enough to adapt in any AI tool

AI Skills Developed

Synthesizes concepts introduced in Amplify, Core, and Illuminate, names them explicitly for the first time

Prompt anatomy Prompt portability Output evaluation Reflective practice

Why It Encourages Return Visits

A grant narrative needs a different prompt than a board report. A two-person organization needs different context than a fifty-person one. Coach does not repeat itself because the work does not repeat itself. A successful session produces something worth sharing, and Leap is where that sharing happens.

Value to SpringboardAI

Coach is where training transfer happens. It applies the same instructional sequence as the live session, try first, understand after, to real work the leader already needs to do. Each session produces a visible artifact and a portable prompt. The field scan found training is only effective when repeated, contextual, and tied to real tasks. Coach is built around that finding.

Considerations

Coach produces drafts, not final documents. All output should be reviewed for accuracy before use.

Describing organizational context conversationally can inadvertently include sensitive information. Leaders should be mindful of what they share in the session.

Optional Future Expansion

Saved prompt library personal to each organization Session history and progress tracking Pathway to Leap: leaders whose Coach sessions produce exceptional results are invited to submit to the community

Concept 05

Leap

One great idea, shared. The community gains momentum.

Agentic Workflow

Submission → AI Review → Human Review → Knowledge Library → Coach + Community

Overview

Leap is the platform's agentic AI concept. AI agents that act without waiting to be asked represent one of the most significant shifts underway in how knowledge work is organized. Leap's agent receives submissions, scores them against defined criteria, generates feedback, and routes approved entries through human review. Approved entries feed into Coach and appear on a public forum where leaders browse wins from peers in similar organizations.

Opportunity

Generic AI examples do not resonate with nonprofit leaders. A housing organization learns differently from a housing example than from a corporate one. No existing platform captures real, sector-specific AI wins at scale and makes them retrievable by context. Leap builds that resource while teaching the skills required to contribute to it.

Submitting real work raises legitimate concerns: confidentiality, rejection, and competitive exposure. Leap addresses each structurally. A confidentiality check runs before any submission enters the queue. Returned submissions are framed as coaching. A sector that learns together moves faster than one where knowledge stays siloed.

How It Works

  • Complete a structured submission form: organization context, challenge faced, AI tool used, prompt, artifact, and outcome
  • Stories and experiences without a formal prompt are accepted
  • Submission triggers the AI agent, which scores the entry and returns one of three outcomes: approved, returned for revision with specific feedback, or discarded with explanation
  • Leaders whose submissions are returned receive live prompt-quality coaching through the feedback itself
  • Approved submissions pass to a human curator, then enter the Leap forum and a searchable database tagged for contextual retrieval by Coach
  • Coach draws on the Leap database to surface contextually relevant examples: a two-person housing nonprofit gets examples from organizations that look like them

AI Skills Developed

Most advanced concept in the set, introduces agentic AI and community knowledge systems

Agentic AI Prompt quality and construction RAG at scale Responsible AI use Human oversight

Why It Encourages Return Visits

Leaders return to see if their submission was approved. They return to browse new entries from peers. They return when Coach surfaces a relevant example from the database. They return to submit their next win. Leap creates four distinct return triggers where most platform assets create one.

Value to SpringboardAI

Leap makes the platform a living resource rather than a static tool set. Each submission strengthens Coach. A leader who submits has moved from consumer to contributor, the deepest form of AI adoption. Every approved submission is a documented behavior change traceable to training. That is evidence funders can act on and SpringboardAI can build from.

Considerations

A confidentiality check runs before any submission enters the review queue, but leaders carry primary responsibility for what they submit.

The database will initially be sparse. A seeding strategy, curated submissions from trusted organizations before public launch, is recommended.

Submissions should be subject to a clear retention and deletion policy. Leaders should be able to remove their contributions at any time.

Optional Future Expansion

Cohort-based sharing among organizations in the same training session Analytics dashboard showing which use cases generate the most wins across the sector Funder-facing view of aggregate AI adoption patterns across grantee organizations

Future Design Recommendation

Learning Outcomes and Assessment

A shared outcomes framework would help SpringboardAI evaluate genuine capability growth rather than activity completion. The following six competencies reflect where nonprofit AI leadership is heading in 2026, durable enough to outlast any specific tool.

AI Literacy

Understand capabilities, limitations, and vocabulary.

Workflow Design

Identify where AI creates the most organizational value.

Prompting and Agents

Move from asking questions to orchestrating work.

Governance and Ethics

Protect trust, privacy, and mission integrity.

Organizational Change

Help staff adopt AI effectively and responsibly.

Strategic Leadership

Decide where AI advances the mission, and where humans must remain central.

Performance-based assessment via Leap: The five concepts build these competencies through authentic, task-based practice. Participants demonstrate understanding by producing artifacts, not completing quizzes. Leap offers the strongest opportunity for performance-based assessment by capturing prompts, revisions, and completed work over time.
